## 1. Verified college overview — official verified

Indian Institute of Technology Madras (IIT Madras / IITM) is a centrally funded statutory Indian Institute of Technology, established in 1959 and recognised as an Institute of National Importance. Its JoSAA institute code is **110**. The main residential campus is at IIT Madras P.O., Sardar Patel Road, Chennai 600036.

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — official verified / unavailable where stated

- Institute of National Importance: verified.
- Autonomous statutory institute governed through its Board of Governors: verified.
- NIRF Engineering: **Rank 1** in 2023, 2024 and 2025; 2025 score **88.72**.
- A conventional institute-specific AICTE, NAAC or programme-level NBA record was not verified and has therefore not been inferred.
- Official campus-area pages conflict: JoSAA says about 650 acres, while the IITM campus page says 617 acres. Both are preserved in `data_conflicts.csv`.

## 3. Admission routes — official verified

On-campus B.Tech, B.S. and integrated dual-degree admission is through **JEE Advanced 2026 and JoSAA 2026**. Indian candidates must satisfy the official JEE Advanced eligibility pathway and the Class XII criterion of 75% aggregate, 65% for SC/ST/PwD, or category-wise top 20 percentile, with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics.

There is no home-state quota, management quota or lateral-entry route in the verified JoSAA route. Category reservation, PwD allocation and female-supernumerary pools apply under JoSAA rules.

**Branch change is discontinued from academic year 2024-25.** Eligible students may still pursue same-department dual-degree/IDDD and entrepreneurship conversion pathways under published CGPA and backlog conditions.

## 4. Branches and intake — official verified

The 2026 JoSAA seat matrix contains **18 on-campus undergraduate programmes and 1,118 total seats**, including 64 female-supernumerary seats. It includes newly introduced Computational Engineering and Mechanics, Instrumentation and Biomedical Engineering, and the 2026 B.S. Mathematics programme. Full category and gender-pool breakup is stored in `college_profile.json`; programme totals are in `branches.csv`.

No historical cutoff is labelled as an official 2026 cutoff.

## 6. Fees and total cost — official verified plus calculated estimates

For the 2026 admission batch, the published semester tuition is ₹1,00,000 for full-fee Indian students. SC/ST/PwD students and General/OBC-NCL/EWS students with family income below ₹1 lakh receive full tuition remission; the ₹1–5 lakh band receives two-thirds tuition remission.

Calculated four-year institute charges for an eight-semester programme, using 2026 components and annual insurance once per year, are approximately:

- Full-fee category: **₹9,39,608**
- ₹1–5 lakh income band: **₹4,06,275**
- Full tuition-remission categories: **₹1,39,608**

These are calculated estimates, not official guaranteed totals, and exclude hostel, mess and future revisions.

For new hostellers in Jul–Nov 2026, mandatory hostel charges are ₹20,750 including a refundable ₹5,000 deposit. The mess advance was not finalised in the published circular, so no complete four-year residential total is presented.

## 8. Placement statistics — official institution and NIRF records

The package includes **60 placement/internship records** covering 2022-23, 2023-24 and 2024-25, plus NIRF UG 4-year and 5-year denominators.

Notable verified records include:

- 2023-24: 1,091 students placed across 256 companies; overall median ₹19.6 LPA and average ₹22 LPA; 235 of 300 PPOs accepted.
- 2024-25 B.Tech branch CTC medians include CSE ₹46 LPA, EE ₹22.5 LPA, Engineering Physics ₹18 LPA, Chemical ₹17.4 LPA and Civil ₹17.2 LPA.
- 2024-25 internships: 724 students across 199 companies; 255 PPOs offered and 202 accepted.
- 2022-23: 1,612 full-time offers from 480+ companies; this is an offer count, not unique students placed.

CTC is not base salary. Branch-specific CTC tables generally do not publish the number registered or placed, so those fields remain null.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — official institution

IIT Madras reports 20 hostels: 15 for male and five for female students. First-year UG students use sharing accommodation; later room type varies. Hostel rooms have LAN, hostels have washing machines and common facilities, and institute buses/mini-autos connect campus zones. The campus includes a central library, departmental/central laboratories, sports facilities, an on-campus hospital, wellness support, security and anti-ragging mechanisms.

Exact 2026 hostel allocation guarantee, room assignment, curfew and mess tender outcome require confirmation.

## 12. Clubs and student activities — institution-associated

Twenty activities are structured. CFI, Shaastra and Saarang have recent official evidence. Static-listed Sangam clubs are retained but marked `unable to verify` where a current event date was not found. CFI's 2025 Open House involved nearly 1,000 students and 60 projects across 26 teams.

## 13. Location and travel — official plus map-derived

The campus is in Adyar/Guindy, Chennai. Official sources place it about 10 km from Chennai Airport and 12–13 km from Chennai Central. Kasturba Nagar is the nearest MRTS station and Guindy the nearest metro, both requiring last-mile travel. Institute transport operates inside the large campus. IIT Madras Research Park and the Taramani/OMR corridor provide nearby technology and startup exposure.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — clearly labelled

Recent public comments consistently identify multiple mess choices but disagree on quality. One detailed 2026 discussion reports approachable seniors and no ragging, but this remains limited anecdotal evidence. Public discussions suggest attendance flexibility while also stressing that lectures matter; no official current attendance regulation was located. Visual tours corroborate the green, extensive residential campus but are not independent audits.

No numeric campus-life score is produced.

## 15. Parent Mode summary

The strongest verified points are top-tier academics, sustained NIRF rank, wide placement participation, residential infrastructure and significant tuition-remission options. The immediate budgeting gap is the 2026 mess charge. Parents should also distinguish CTC from in-hand salary and understand that new programmes lack graduating placement history.

## 16. Student Mode summary

IITM is best suited to students who value academic flexibility, research, innovation teams, large festivals and a demanding peer environment. It may suit less well those relying on a later branch change or wanting a small, centrally located urban campus with minimal internal travel.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

Major risks are recorded in `college_profile.json`: discontinued branch change, missing graduate outcomes for new programmes, pending mess charges, placement-scope limitations, relative-grading workload, limited scholarships and large-campus logistics.
