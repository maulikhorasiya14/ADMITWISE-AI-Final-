## 6. Fees and total cost - Official verified + calculated

### B.Tech calculated institute fee for four years, excluding hostel/mess

- Income above ₹5 lakh / DASA-CIWG/MEA: **₹6.63 lakh**
- Income ₹1-5 lakh: **₹3.30 lakh**
- Income below ₹1 lakh and all SC/ST/PwD: **₹1.63 lakh**

These calculations include the refundable ₹20,000 institute caution deposit and the official recurring/one-time institute fees. They exclude hostel and mess.

### Hostel

- One-time hostel charges: **₹25,500**, including ₹15,000 refundable deposit.
- Fixed hostel establishment + seat rent: **₹25,000 per semester / ₹50,000 per year**.
- First-semester table includes a **₹25,000 mess advance**; the note says students should maintain **₹20,000 credit** at the start of every semester. Actual mess expense is consumption-based, so no four-year mess total is claimed.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality - Student-reported / anecdotal

- Green, large residential campus is a repeated positive theme.
- Hostel quality varies by block; some older rooms are described as basic or congested.
- Coding culture is perceived differently across branches and peer groups, although official technical clubs and CCD training exist.
- Daytime city connectivity is available; late-night travel may be less convenient.
- Evidence is too limited and self-selected for a numeric satisfaction rating.

## 15. Parent Mode summary

NIT Calicut has strong statutory status, a current 2026 seat matrix, official fee circulars and solid overall NIRF outcomes. The main parent-facing checks are the branch choice because branch change is unavailable, category-specific cost, scholarship sanction, hostel/mess budgeting and the absence of branch-specific placement rates.

## 16. Student Mode summary

Choose the branch deliberately, use technical clubs and CCD resources early, and avoid treating recruiter lists or historical ranks as a guarantee. Campus life is broad and residential, but city travel and hostel-block variation should be expected.

## 17. Decision risks

- **High** - Historical cutoffs are not guarantees for 2026. New branches, seat changes, applicant demand and counselling rules can shift ranks.
- **High** - JoSAA and CSAB rank semantics differ: CSAB PDF ranks are All India CRL even for category rows, while JoSAA reserved-category rows use category ranks.
- **High** - The 2024 AI quota label requires verification before predictive modelling.
- **Medium** - Mathematics and Computing is new for 2026 and lacks historical cutoff evidence.
- **High** - Branch change is unavailable for the 2026 batch, increasing the importance of branch choice at admission.
- **High** - Official placement outcomes are overall and cannot be safely converted into branch-specific placement probabilities.
- **Medium** - Hostel and mess costs can change and mess expenditure is not a fixed annual fee.
- **Medium** - Campus is about 22 km from Kozhikode city; late-night travel may be less convenient.
- **High** - Scholarships require separate application, verification and sanction; availability and benefits are not guaranteed.

## 18. Data gaps

- Official completed 2026 JoSAA/CSAB cutoff rounds are not available in this package; 2023-2025 are historical evidence only.
- The uploaded 2024 JoSAA subset includes 28 rows with blank opening and closing ranks; these are documented as unavailable combinations and omitted from numeric cutoff exports.
- Current authoritative AICTE institute ID, UGC category and NAAC grade/score/validity were not verified and remain null.
- Exact NBA validity dates were not obtained for Engineering Physics; Biotechnology renewal after 2023-2026 is unclear.
- Branch-specific placement percentages, eligible/registered counts, PPOs, internships and stipend data were not available in the NIRF source.
- The official placement notice’s exact recruitment year is not exposed in the retrieved page text.
- Scholarship amount, income limit, deadline and documents are incomplete for most cycle-specific schemes.
- Mess cost is an advance/consumption-based item, so a reliable four-year hostel-and-mess total was not calculated.
- Company-specific role and branch-eligibility records were not published in the official recruiter notice.
- Qualitative evidence is limited and self-selected; it cannot establish a campus-wide numerical satisfaction score.
- Mathematics and Computing is new in the 2026 seat matrix and has no historical 2023-2025 cutoff rows in the uploaded data.
