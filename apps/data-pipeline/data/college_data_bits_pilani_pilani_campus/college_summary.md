## 3. Admission routes — Official verified

The main domestic first-degree route is BITSAT followed by BITS' own preference and iteration process. For most B.E. programmes, the published eligibility is at least 75% aggregate in Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics, with at least 60% in each subject. Environmental & Sustainability Engineering also permits the specified PCB pathway, with additional mathematics-proficiency conditions. Separate official routes include direct admission for eligible board toppers, International Student Admission, and listed collaborative pathways.

Historical scores are evidence for 2026 planning only. They are not official 2026 cutoffs.

## 6. Fees and total cost — Official verified + calculated

For 2026-27 Pilani admissions, the published semester tuition is **INR 291,500**, the admission fee is **INR 64,100**, the hostel fee is **INR 36,000 per semester**, and the mess/electricity advance is **INR 10,000 per semester**. The published tuition schedule rises in later years.

- **Calculated first-year billed/advanced cash outflow:** INR 766,775
- **Calculated four-year scheduled tuition only:** INR 2,513,000
- **Unavailable:** A defensible four-year institutional total, because future hostel/mess rates and adjustable advances are not fixed.

These calculations exclude personal travel, laptop, clothing, entertainment and optional summer-term costs.

## 7. Scholarships — Official verified / conditional

The 2026 page states a tuition-blind provision for eligible top-500 BITSAT rankers with annual family income up to INR 20 lakh, with a 100% tuition waiver and a minimum CGPA 7.5 renewal condition. Institute Merit and Merit-cum-Need bands are also published semester-wise. The detailed MCN page states an income threshold below INR 15 lakh; the operative 2026-27 notice and documents should be manually confirmed.

Other recorded support includes the Students Aid Fund, Golden 76 Scholarship, a BITS–BITSAA travel fellowship, and PM-Vidyalaxmi loan-interest support. None is represented as guaranteed.

## 12. Clubs and student activities — Institution-published

Twenty organizations are recorded. Most come from a static official listing and are therefore marked **unable to verify** for current activity. Astro Club, Coding Club and Team Anant have more recent institution-published evidence and are marked **recently active**.

## 13. Location and travel — Official + approximate

Pilani is a remote residential campus. Official guidance places it roughly 200 km from Delhi and 220 km from Jaipur. Chirawa (about 16 km) and Loharu (about 24 km) are the commonly cited nearby railway access points. Airport road travel can take several hours. Exact coordinates and local travel-time estimates require map verification.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — D/E evidence

Seventeen public student-experience sources were reviewed.

- **Senior–junior culture:** generally approachable and mentoring-oriented, with minimal-ragging reports; localized club-hierarchy concerns remain anecdotal.
- **Academics:** strong peers and self-learning, but comparison pressure and dual-degree workload can be significant.
- **Hostels/mess:** fully residential; room and mess satisfaction varies, and heat is a repeated concern.
- **Campus life:** strong residential social ecosystem and many clubs, with selective inductions in some groups.
- **Coding:** peer-learning evidence is positive, while a recent thread questions competitive-programming depth; evidence is conflicting.
- **Locality:** close-knit but remote, hot and less convenient for spontaneous city access.

These themes are not official facts or representative survey results.

## 15. Parent Mode summary

Prioritize the high published cost, conditional scholarships, exact placement-report scope, remote travel, official residential/medical systems, and unresolved intake/hostel details. Do not budget on the assumption of receiving a scholarship.

## 16. Student Mode summary

The strongest fit is for a student seeking high-calibre peers, a residential community, clubs and self-directed career opportunities. It is a weaker fit for someone who needs metropolitan access, low fees, uniformly premium hostels or a low-pressure academic environment.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

1. High and escalating fees.
2. No public branch-wise sanctioned intake.
3. Newest placement report is university-wide, not Pilani-only.
4. Promotional placement wording conflicts with the exact 2023-24 campus rate.
5. New programmes have limited historical evidence.
6. Remote location and heat may materially affect fit.
7. Scholarship and hostel operational details require current notices.
