## 1. Verified college overview — official verified

NIT Tiruchirappalli (institute code **226**) is a Central Government National Institute of Technology, established in **1964** as Regional Engineering College, Tiruchirappalli. It became NIT in 2003 and an Institution of National Importance in 2007. The main residential campus is at Thuvakudi on the Trichy–Thanjavur highway. [SRC001, SRC007, SRC008]

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — official verified / unavailable

- NIRF Engineering: **Rank 9** in 2025 (score 68.14), 2024 (66.88) and 2023 (69.71). [SRC009–SRC011]
- B.Tech Mechanical Engineering: NBA accreditation reported for **2023–2028**. [SRC013]
- Current NAAC grade and a complete current NBA programme roster were not verified and remain null.

## 3. Admission routes — official verified

B.Tech admissions use JEE Main through JoSAA; CSAB Special handles remaining vacancies. B.Arch uses the applicable JEE Main architecture route. DASA, ICCR and Study in India routes are also listed. The official 2026 profile states **no branch-change policy after admission**. [SRC001, SRC008]

## 4. Branches and intake — official verified

The 2026 JoSAA seat matrix contains **10 undergraduate programmes** and **1,038 seats**. `branches.csv` preserves the official programme names and normalized codes; `college_profile.json` contains quota and gender-pool seat totals. Female-only pools exist, but the 2026 matrix reports zero additional female supernumerary seats. [SRC001]

## 5. Historical cutoffs — official verified

Collected **4,275** rows:

- JoSAA: 4,071 rows for 2023, 2024 and 2025.
- CSAB Special/DASA 2025: 204 rows across Rounds 1–3.
- Year counts: 2023: 1,414, 2024: 1,212, 2025: 1,649.

JoSAA reserved-category ranks are retained as category ranks. The attached CSAB PDFs explicitly state that their ranks are All India CRL. **8** JoSAA combinations with blank opening and closing ranks were excluded because no cutoff value was published. No row is labelled as an official 2026 cutoff. [SRC002–SRC006]

## 6. Fees and total cost — official verified + calculated

For the 2026 B.Tech standard category, tuition is INR 62,500 per semester. The calculated institute-only four-year amount is **INR 663,750**, consisting of INR 500,000 tuition, INR 128,450 in four annual-fee blocks and INR 35,300 one-time fees. This excludes hostel and mess. SC/ST/all-PwD students have zero tuition. Income-based remission provides full tuition remission below INR 1 lakh and two-thirds remission for INR 1–5 lakh. [SRC014]

The first odd-semester 2026-27 hostel total for a newly admitted resident is **INR 59,950**, including a five-month INR 24,000 mess advance. It is not annualized. [SRC015]

## 7. Scholarships — institution-published

Seven structured records are included. The RECT’78 Alumni Scholarship is around INR 45,000 per scholar per year and selects one girl and one boy, subject to academic, need and renewal conditions. The official page and detailed PDF show conflicting deadlines, which is flagged. [SRC019–SRC021]

## 8. Placement statistics — official / institution-published

`placements.csv` includes **33 records** for 2021-22, 2022-23 and 2023-24. The placement cell reports percentages of registered UG students; NIRF separately reports graduating students, students placed, higher studies and median salary. These denominators must not be merged. For 2023-24, NIRF reports 1,029 graduating in minimum time, 791 placed, median salary INR 14.35 lakh and 158 going to higher studies. [SRC012, SRC017]

## 9. Recruiters — institution-published

Thirty companies shown in the official 2025-26 UG placement brochure are recorded. Their appearance in the brochure does not prove a completed drive, role, branch eligibility, selection count or package for that year. [SRC018]

## 10. Company-to-skill guidance — calculated/general guidance

General skill clusters are provided for software, semiconductor/electronics and finance/technology recruiters. They are not presented as company-specific NITT eligibility rules.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — institution-published

The official hostel page states **22 boys’ hostels, 6 girls’ hostels and capacity for 6,966 residents**, with one hostel under construction. The campus profile lists an institute hospital, ambulance, BHEL referral, library, computing facilities, sports centre, pool, bank, post office, transport and other residential services. [SRC001, SRC016, SRC024]

## 12. Clubs and student activities — institution-published

Twenty clubs were selected from the current official 2025–2027 faculty-advisor roster. Their official status is verified, but recruitment method and recent activity frequency require manual checking. [SRC022, SRC023]

## 13. Location and travel — official verified + conflict

Official sources place the campus on the Trichy–Thanjavur highway and describe a roughly 30-minute bus journey from Tiruchirappalli. A distance conflict of **17 km versus about 22 km** is preserved. Current map measurements were not guessed. [SRC001, SRC007]

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — anecdotal

Nine public qualitative sources were reviewed. Recurring themes include strong fests and a large residential peer environment, very hot weather, mixed hostel/mess experiences, distance from the city and concerns about gender-differentiated curfew rules. These reports are self-selected and are not official facts. [SRC101–SRC109]

## 15. Parent Mode summary

Strengths: stable high NIRF engineering rank, national peer group, strong official placement indicators, broad campus infrastructure and meaningful fee remission. Verify the exact branch, quota, hostel rules, full residential cost and current placement scope before deciding.

## 16. Student Mode summary

The institute is suited to students who value technical clubs, fests, a residential campus and a strong placement environment. Consider branch workload, hot weather, city distance, lack of branch change and current hostel/curfew rules.

## 17. Decision risks

See `college_profile.json` for structured risks. Highest-priority risks are historical-not-guaranteed cutoffs, non-comparable placement denominators, no branch change, missing final current placement report and incomplete annual residential cost.
