## 1. Verified college overview — Official verified / institution-published

BITS Pilani's K K Birla Goa Campus is a private, fully residential constituent campus of Birla Institute of Technology & Science, Pilani, a deemed-to-be university. The campus was established in 2004 and is institution-published as occupying approximately 180 acres at Zuarinagar/Sancoale in Goa. [S001, S006]

The university—not the Goa campus separately—holds Institution of Eminence status and is the entity represented in UGC, NAAC and NIRF records.

## 2. Accreditation — Official verified, university-wide

- NAAC: **A++**, CGPA **3.68**, stated validity 29 November 2024 to 28 November 2029. [S002]
- NIRF Engineering: rank **11** in 2025 (score 67.02), **20** in 2024 (63.04), and **25** in 2023 (59.52). [S003–S005]
- Current campus-specific AICTE institute ID and programme-wise NBA validity: **unavailable / not verified**.

## 3. Admission routes — Official verified

Domestic first-degree admissions use BITSAT 2026. The current rule requires at least 75% aggregate in the prescribed PCM/PCB subjects and at least 60% in each relevant subject. BITS states that it does not use JEE Main/JEE Advanced for this route, does not have a management quota, and does not apply state/category reservation in the usual counselling sense. [S007, S008]

Other official pathways include direct admission for eligible board toppers and the International Student Admission route. [S013, S014]

## 4. Branches and intake — Official verified / unavailable

Nine current Goa-campus B.E. programmes were verified for 2026-27: Chemical, EEE, Mechanical, Computer Science, Electronics and Instrumentation, Electronics and Communication, Mathematics and Computing, Electronics and Computer Engineering, and Environmental and Sustainability Engineering. [S007]

## 6. Fees and total cost — Official verified / calculated

For 2026-27, semester tuition is INR 291,500 and annual tuition is INR 583,000. The admission fee is INR 64,100, caution deposit INR 3,000, annual hostel fee represented from the published semester schedule is INR 72,000, and annual mess/electricity advance is INR 20,000. [S010]

The calculated first-year billed/advanced cash outflow is **INR 766,775**. Published scheduled tuition over four years totals **INR 2,513,000**. This is not a fixed all-inclusive four-year cost because future hostel/mess revisions and adjustable advances are not published.

## 7. Scholarships — Official verified / potentially applicable

Official merit awards cover 100% tuition for the top 2% and 50% for the next 1% under semester merit criteria. MCN slabs publish 100%, 50%, 25% and 10% tuition waivers for specified merit-cum-need bands. Top-500 BITSAT ranker support can cover tuition for the normal four-year duration subject to income and CGPA conditions. [S011, S012]

All awards are competitive and conditional. The current semester's income-document rules and deadlines must be checked after admission.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — Institution-published / student-reported

Official pages describe a fully residential campus, furnished rooms, hostel internet, a Student Activity Centre, gym and indoor/outdoor sports, shopping and laundry services, banking, library and a medical centre with emergency support and referral arrangements. [S006, S015–S017]

A recent student thread reports first-year sharing with later single-room allocation, which is more specific than the official overview and requires verification for 2026-27. [SX01]

## 12. Clubs and student activities — Institution-published

NIRMAAN, ERC and CEL have official campus pages. The campus also officially documents the Quark, Waves and Spree ecosystems, BITScan, annual magazine, PMP and historical TEDx activity. [S020–S023, S026–S027]

Only NIRMAAN and the entrepreneurship ecosystem had clearly recent public activity evidence. Other activity statuses are deliberately marked “unable to verify” or “possibly dormant”.

## 13. Location and travel — Official / map-derived

The campus is on NH 17B Bypass Road at Zuarinagar/Sancoale. The official campus description places it about 10 km south of Vasco-da-Gama, 20 km north of Margao and 7 km east of Dabolim airport. [S006]

Map-derived estimates place Vasco railway station and bus terminal roughly 10 km away and Goa Medical College around 19 km away. Actual travel time varies with route, tourism traffic and monsoon conditions. [S029]

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — Student-reported

Repeated positive themes include residential peer life, visible club/department culture, festivals, technical activity and access to a wide recruiter ecosystem. Repeated concerns include strict grading, CGPA-sensitive opportunities, selective club inductions, high fees, branch-dependent software eligibility and the age of publicly available campus placement outcomes. [SX01–SX12]

These are qualitative signals, not official facts or numeric ratings.

## 15. Parent Mode summary

The strongest verified positives are formal admissions transparency, official historical cutoffs, established residential facilities, airport proximity and historically high overall placement percentages. The biggest unresolved parent-facing risks are total cost, scholarship uncertainty, dated Goa-specific placement reporting, missing branch-wise intake and incomplete hostel operating details.

## 16. Student Mode summary

The campus appears best suited to students who value a residential peer environment, clubs/festivals, self-directed learning and broad career optionality. Students focused on a specific software role should verify branch eligibility rather than assume every company permits every B.E. programme.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

Primary risks are high fees, uncertain scholarship realization, dated campus-specific placements, no public branch-wise placement table, non-circuit-to-software access uncertainty, grading pressure and potentially mismatched hostel expectations.

## 18. Data gaps

Major gaps include the official seat matrix, current AICTE/NBA programme records, final 2026 cutoffs, post-2022-23 Goa placement outcomes, branch-level placement data, current hostel capacity/occupancy and reliable recent activity evidence for many clubs.
