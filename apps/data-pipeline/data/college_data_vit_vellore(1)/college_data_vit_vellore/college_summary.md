## 1. Verified college overview — Official verified

Vellore Institute of Technology is a private deemed-to-be university established in 1984 as Vellore Engineering College. The Vellore main campus is at Katpadi–Thiruvalam Road, Vellore, Tamil Nadu 632014. Current official infrastructure material describes a **372-acre** campus; an older official travel/event page described approximately 370 acres. [S001–S004, S024, S038]

## 2. Accreditation — Official verified / institution scope

VIT reports NAAC **A++**, CGPA **3.66**, cycle 4. NIRF Engineering ranks were **16** in 2025 with score 65.25, **11** in 2024 with score 66.22, and **11** in 2023 with score 66.59 under institution ID IR-E-U-0490. [S003, S006–S008]

VIT also publishes an AICTE mandatory disclosure and a 2025-26 Extension of Approval document. Current 2026-27 programme-level approval details and programme-wise NBA validity were not fully verified. [S009, S010]

## 3. Admission routes — Official verified

Domestic B.Tech admission uses **VITEEE 2026**, followed by centralized online counselling. Candidates choose campus, programme and fee category during counselling, and allotment depends on rank and availability. The official FAQ states that VITEEE is mandatory; JEE Main, SAT and board marks do not replace it for the regular domestic B.Tech route. VIT states that there is no management quota and no B.Tech lateral entry. [S011, S012]

The general PCM eligibility is 60% aggregate under the official rules, with specified relaxations. PCB candidates are restricted to the programmes explicitly listed on the admission portal. Separate NRI/foreign and STARS pathways have their own rules. [S011, S017]

## 4. Branches and intake — Official verified / historical intake

Nineteen current Vellore-campus B.Tech programmes were verified for 2026-27. These include Biotechnology, Chemical, Civil, Civil with L&T, CSE and six current CSE variants, EEE, Electronics and Computer Engineering, ECE, E&I, Electronics–VLSI, Health Sciences and Technology, IT and Mechanical Engineering. [S011]

The latest fully extracted programme-wise sanctioned-intake table is the **AICTE 2024-25 mandatory disclosure**, not a 2026 counselling seat matrix. Seventeen exact current-programme matches carry historical intake values; Civil with L&T and Electronics and Computer Engineering remain null because no exact reliable match was made. [S009]

Examples of 2024-25 sanctioned intake include CSE 1,080, ECE 480, Mechanical 480, IT 420, CSE Data Science 300, and Biotechnology 180. Category-wise, fee-category-wise and 2026 seat distributions were not published.

## 5. Historical cutoffs — Unavailable

VIT does **not publish stable official branch-wise historical closing-rank tables**. Its own 2026 FAQ says the rank range for a programme can only be tentatively understood after a candidate receives a rank and participates in counselling. `cutoffs.csv` therefore contains the required schema with **zero data rows**, and no third-party prediction is imported. [S011]

The 2026 counselling portal is account-based and does not expose a public complete branch/category closing-rank dataset. [S012]

## 6. Fees and total cost — Official / calculated / unresolved

The public B.Tech academic-fee subsection is explicitly labelled **2025-26**, even though the surrounding UG fee page includes 2026-27 sections for other programmes. [S013]

- Group A tuition: INR 1,73,000 per year; calculated four-year tuition INR 6,92,000.
- Civil Engineering with L&T: INR 2,07,000 per year; calculated four-year tuition INR 8,28,000.
- Group B tuition: INR 1,95,000 per year; calculated four-year tuition INR 7,80,000.
- Refundable B.Tech caution deposit: INR 3,000.

These are historical scheduled-tuition calculations and are **not official 2026-27 totals**.

Official 2026-27 first-year hostel schedules publish combined room-and-mess packages:

- Men: INR 1,50,700 to INR 2,64,800 total first-year payment.
- Women UG: INR 1,56,200 to INR 2,79,400 total first-year payment.

The hostel totals include a non-refundable admission fee and refundable caution deposit; room and mess are not consistently separable into the requested annual columns. [S014, S015]

A dependable four-year all-inclusive institutional total cannot be calculated until the 2026-27 B.Tech fee-category table and future hostel revisions are available.

## 7. Scholarships — Official verified / conditional

The current public institutional scholarship page lists GVSDP VITEEE-rank slabs: 100% tuition waiver for ranks 1–10, 75% for 11–50, 50% for 51–100 and 25% for 101–500, subject to the current award conditions. The displayed schedule must be reconfirmed for 2026-27 before import. [S016]

Other retained records include the Smt. Rajeswari Viswanathan Memorial Scholarship, institutional merit awards, sports scholarships, STARS and potentially applicable National Scholarship Portal schemes. STARS can support eligible rural government-school toppers with academic, hostel and basic medical expenses under current rules. [S016, S017, S040]

No scholarship is presented as guaranteed.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — Official / student-reported

The current official hostel page states that Vellore Campus has **32 hostel blocks: 21 for men and 11 for women**, serving over 30,000 residents annually. A 2024-25 mandatory disclosure recorded 24 blocks and over 22,000 residents, indicating either expansion or stale documentation. [S019, S009]

Official facilities include AC/non-AC sharing options, Wi-Fi, mechanized laundry, security, multiple mess systems, food outlets, sports and other student services. Freshers are expected to live on campus unless a parent or guardian stays with them. [S011, S018, S019, S022, S042]

The health centre publishes inpatient beds, pharmacy, ambulances, laboratory and physiotherapy support; well-being and disability-support services are also officially listed. [S020, S021, S023]

Student accounts consistently indicate that room size, block age, bathroom arrangement, walking distance and mess quality vary materially. [SX01, SX04, SX07, SX09, SX12, SX13, SX15, SX16]

## 13. Location and travel — Official / map-derived

The campus is on Katpadi–Thiruvalam Road. Katpadi Junction is approximately 3 km away, while Chennai International Airport is approximately 135 km away and commonly requires a multi-hour road or rail transfer. [S002, S038]

Vellore New Bus Stand, CMC Vellore and local city-centre distances are map-derived estimates. Ranipet/SIPCOT provides a nearby industrial ecosystem; Chennai and Bengaluru technology markets require intercity travel. [S039]

No direct metro serves the campus.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — Student-reported

The strongest repeated positive signals are infrastructure, a large peer ecosystem, clubs, events and access to a broad centralized placement network. The strongest repeated negative signals are crowding, competition, high total cost, block-dependent hostel/mess conditions and uncertainty around attendance administration.

The evidence contains no numeric campus-culture rating. Each theme records source count, date range and whether reports agree or conflict in `campus_reality.json`.

## 15. Parent Mode summary

VIT Vellore offers established infrastructure, official scholarship pathways, strong railway connectivity, a large residential system, substantial health support and a broad employer ecosystem. The largest unresolved risks are the absence of official cutoff tables, unresolved 2026 B.Tech category fees, institution-level rather than campus-only placement reporting, premium hostel costs and the scale of the cohort.

## 16. Student Mode summary

The college appears best suited to students prepared to navigate a large, competitive opportunity ecosystem proactively. Clubs, coding, technical teams, projects and external internship applications matter. Students seeking a small cohort, low fees, relaxed attendance or highly personalised administration may find the environment less suitable.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

Primary decision risks are missing official cutoffs, unresolved 2026 tuition/category mapping, placement-scope ambiguity, premium residence costs, attendance uncertainty, large-cohort competition, block-dependent hostel experience and assuming every branch has equal software-role access.

## 18. Data gaps
