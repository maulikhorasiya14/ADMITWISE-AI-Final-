## 1. Verified college overview — official verified

Malaviya National Institute of Technology Jaipur, commonly called **MNIT Jaipur**, was established in 1963 as Malaviya Regional Engineering College Jaipur, became an NIT in 2002 and an Institute of National Importance in 2007. It is fully funded by the Ministry of Education. The JoSAA institute code is **202**. The main campus is on Jawahar Lal Nehru Marg, Malviya Nagar, Jaipur 302017. Sources: SRC001, SRC024, SRC025.

## 3. Admission routes — official verified

Regular B.Tech and B.Arch admission is through JEE Main followed by JoSAA/CSAB. The 2026 matrix has Home State and Other State seats, category reservations and gender-neutral/female-only pools, but **zero female-supernumerary seats**. DASA and other government-sponsored foreign-student routes are separately documented; DASA 2026 eligibility must be checked from the current brochure. Branch change is not permitted according to the JoSAA institute profile.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — anecdotal and conflicting

- **Campus life:** Public student/vlog evidence and official event records indicate an active residential and club environment.
- **Hostels:** Newer/first-year accommodation is described positively in some sources, while a separate protest-context discussion reports allotment and administrative restrictions. Conditions likely vary by hostel and year.
- **Coding culture:** Several recent student comments describe coding as self-driven and weaker than applicants may expect. This conflicts with the institute’s active technical-club ecosystem and major recruiter presence, so it should not be treated as a college-wide fact.
- **Academics and senior-junior culture:** Evidence is too limited for a reliable college-wide conclusion.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

1. **Historical cutoffs are not official 2026 cutoffs.** Severity: high. Mitigation: Use them only for trend bands and update after official 2026 rounds.
2. **Engineering Physics and Mathematics and Computing lack historical cutoff/placement records in the supplied period.** Severity: high. Mitigation: Do not extrapolate branch-specific ROI from older branches without clear assumptions.
3. **Software-focused students in core branches may face branch-dependent role eligibility and a self-driven coding environment.** Severity: medium-high. Mitigation: Verify recruiter eligibility, build CS fundamentals early and use technical clubs/projects.
4. **Placement rates fluctuate and use registered-student denominators.** Severity: medium-high. Mitigation: Compare branch medians and registered/placed counts across all three years; do not rely only on highest package.
5. **Hostel capacity does not guarantee allotment.** Severity: medium. Mitigation: Confirm 2026 allotment policy and retain an off-campus contingency.
6. **Mess amount is an advance adjusted at actual.** Severity: medium. Mitigation: Budget with a contingency rather than treating ₹25,000 per semester as a final fixed cost.
7. **Most programme-level NBA information is outdated.** Severity: medium. Mitigation: Verify current certificates before using accreditation in comparisons.
8. **Scholarships are conditional and cycle-specific.** Severity: medium. Mitigation: Check income/category rules and official deadlines; never treat a match as guaranteed.
9. **Student-experience evidence is conflicting and may not represent all batches.** Severity: medium. Mitigation: Speak with multiple current students from the exact branch and hostel before deciding.
