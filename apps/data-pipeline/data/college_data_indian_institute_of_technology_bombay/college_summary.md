## 1. Verified college overview — official verified

IIT Bombay is an autonomous statutory institute and deemed university established in 1958 at Powai, Mumbai. It was declared an Institute of National Importance in 1961. Its JoSAA institute code is **102**.

The package uses the Powai campus only. No other institution or campus is mixed into the records.

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — official verified / unavailable

- NIRF Engineering 2025: Rank 3, score 83.65.
- NIRF Engineering 2024: Rank 3, score 83.09.
- NIRF Engineering 2023: Rank 3, score 80.74.
- NIRF institution ID: IR-E-U-0306.
- Current institute-specific AICTE approval, NAAC grade and programme-level NBA validity were not verified and remain null.

## 3. Admission route — official verified

UG admission to the listed programmes is through JEE Advanced and JoSAA under All India quota pools. The 2026 matrix includes OPEN, GEN-EWS, OBC-NCL, SC, ST and PwD categories and female-supernumerary seats.

**Branch-change warning:** IIT Bombay states that there is no provision for branch change for JoSAA-admitted programmes from academic year 2023-24.

## 4. Programmes and intake — official verified

The 2026 JoSAA profile lists **16 programmes**, with **1,204 seat capacity plus 159 female-supernumerary seats = 1,363 seats**. Detailed category and gender-pool records are in `college_profile.json`; programme-level capacity is in `branches.csv`.

## 6. Fees and total cost — official verified + calculated

For 2026-27 new UG entrants, the official academic first-semester total for Indian nationals paying full tuition is INR 134,150, including INR 10,000 refundable deposits. The first-semester hostel total is INR 19,950 plus INR 22,500 mess advance.

At unchanged 2026-27 rates, the calculated four-year full-tuition institutional, hostel and mess cost is approximately **INR 1,195,800**. This is not an official fixed programme total and excludes personal costs, travel and future revisions.

SC/ST/PwD students are exempt from tuition. Income-based remission is subject to verification.

## 7. Scholarships — institution-published

Eight structured scholarship/remission records are included: three MCM income slabs, Women Merit Scholarship, SC/ST free messing and pocket allowance, full tuition remission, two-thirds tuition remission and statutory SC/ST/PwD tuition exemption. All matching language is non-guaranteed.

## 8. Placement statistics — institution-published

The package includes 12 records across 2022-23, 2023-24 and 2024-25, preserving programme-level denominators and overall outcomes. Official 2024-25 figures include 2,469 registered students, 2,040 participants, 1,422 accepted offers, INR 26.45 LPA average CTC and INR 20 LPA median CTC.

The 2022-23 INR 16.66 LPA salary measure is left out of the median field because official documents label it inconsistently; see conflict C002.

## 9. Recruiters — historical institution-associated evidence

## 10. Company-to-skill guidance — general guidance

Eight general role-category roadmaps are included in `college_profile.json`. They are labelled confidence F and are not company-specific IIT Bombay hiring requirements.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — official/institution-associated

Official pages document a residential campus, hostel internet, messes, laundry, recreation, sports, library, on-campus hospital and wellness support. Exact 2026 room allocation, occupancy and building quality are not guaranteed.

## 12. Clubs and student activities — institution-associated

Twenty current official or institution-associated organisations are structured. Several have dated 2026 activity, including WnCC, ERC and the Design Club. Organisations lacking a dated event remain marked for recency verification.

## 13. Location and travel — official + map-derived

The campus is in Powai. Kanjur Marg is the nearest suburban railway station, and Terminal 2 of Mumbai airport is officially described as about 10 km away. Coordinates, travel times and city-centre distance are map-derived approximations.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — anecdotal

Eight public sources were reviewed. Repeated themes include strong opportunity density, academic pressure, a central hostel culture, building-to-building variation and broad extracurricular choice. The evidence is not strong enough for numeric campus-life, mess, hostel or faculty scores.

## 15. Parent Mode summary

IIT Bombay has authoritative 2026 admissions, seat and fee data, strong historical placement outcomes and extensive on-campus facilities. The current calculated four-year institutional/hostel/mess cost for a full-tuition-paying Indian student is about INR 11.96 lakh before personal expenses and future revisions. Scholarships and remissions can materially reduce tuition but are not guaranteed. The main unresolved parent-facing issues are exact hostel room allocation, final 2025-26 placement outcomes, company-wise employment details and the campus-area source conflict.

## 16. Student Mode summary

IIT Bombay offers 16 JoSAA programmes, no branch change from 2023-24, a strong technical/cultural ecosystem, a residential campus and broad placement sectors. Students should choose the allotted branch on its own merits, use minors/honours and clubs for exploration, and build role-specific skills rather than assuming every recruiter is open to every branch. Hostel experience varies across buildings and workload can be demanding.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

- Do not select a branch assuming a later branch change.
- Do not treat placement averages or CTC as personal guarantees.
- Do not assume uniform hostel quality or a specific room type.
- Do not assume scholarships are automatic.
- Do not treat isolated online opinions as campus-wide facts.

## 18. Data gaps

- Official 2026 JoSAA cutoffs are not yet available; historical 2023-2025 cutoffs are provided without prediction labels.
- Final 2025-26 placement report is not publicly available in the collected evidence.
- Company-wise current recruiters, roles, branch eligibility, selection counts and packages are not publicly complete.
- Current AICTE, NAAC and programme-level NBA applicability/records were not verified.
- Uniform hostel room occupancy, allotment guarantee, curfew and current building-wise charges/rules are not fully documented.
- Current activity dates and recruitment processes for some clubs require manual recheck.
- Independent student evidence is insufficient for numeric campus-life, mess, faculty-accessibility or senior-junior scores.
- Live map travel times and Mumbai public-transport routes can change.

## 19. Conflicts
