## 1. Verified college overview — Official verified
Indian Institute of Information Technology Lucknow is a PPP-model Institute of National Importance established in 2015. Its permanent campus is at Chak Ganjaria/C.G. City, Lucknow. JoSAA institute code: **315**.

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — Official verified / unavailable
Institute-of-National-Importance and autonomous status are verified. Current NIRF rank, NAAC validity and programme-wise NBA status were not conclusively verified and remain null.

## 3. Admission routes — Official verified
B.Tech admission is through JEE Main and JoSAA, followed by CSAB Special for vacancies. The 2026 profile shows All India allocation, central reservation/PwD pools and no branch-change facility. DASA/CIWG applies only under current rules and availability.

## 4. Branches and intake — Official verified
The 2026 matrix contains four B.Tech programmes: CS, IT, CSAI and CSB. Each has 60 seats (48 gender-neutral + 12 female-only), for **240 seats**. The official matrix shows **zero female-supernumerary seats**.

## 5. Historical cutoffs — Official verified
The package contains **868** validated cutoff records for 2023–2025: **841 JoSAA** and **27 CSAB Special**. JoSAA category ranks remain separate from CSAB All India CRL. **4** blank-rank JoSAA combinations were excluded. CSAB rows by round: Round 1 = 0, Round 2 = 17, Round 3 = 10.

## 6. Fees and total cost — Institution-published / calculated
The detailed schedule displayed through the current JoSAA profile is labelled for older batches and is subject to revision. The calculated four-year total is ₹1,193,800 under the published base hostel charge, including the stated mess deposit and eight semester mess charges. Occupancy variants are separately calculated in `fees.csv`. These are **not official final 2026-27 totals**.

## 7. Scholarships — Official/institution-published
Nine potentially applicable schemes are structured, including UP Post Matric, MMVY, FFE, Reliance Foundation and central NSP schemes. Eligibility, amount, documents and deadlines must be verified for the active cycle; no award is guaranteed.

## 8. Placement statistics — Institution-published
The 2025-26 brochure reports B.Tech placement outcomes for 2022-23, 2023-24 and 2024-25. The latest record reports 96.65% placed, average 34.04 LPA, median 30 LPA and highest 145 LPA. Student denominators are not published, so percentage records remain brochure-scope claims rather than independently recomputed rates.

## 9. Recruiters — Institution-published
Thirty recruiters are included from the current placement page and 2025-26 brochure. Logos do not establish a current role, branch eligibility, package or selection count unless separately published.

## 10. Company-to-skill guidance — General career guidance
Software, AI/data and analytics/product preparation roadmaps appear in `college_profile.json`. They are not company-specific verified requirements.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — Institution-published / conflict flagged
The brochure reports a 50-acre campus, accessible academic facilities and a 102-room co-educational hostel. Official 2024 committee minutes separately record severe accommodation shortage, outsourced twin-sharing accommodation and approval of a new 1000-seat hostel. Current year-wise allotment is a major manual-verification item.

## 12. Clubs and student activities — Institution-associated
Nineteen technical, cultural, sports, finance, social and entrepreneurship groups are structured. The 2025-26 brochure also documents Equinox, Infinito, E-Summit, Hackofiesta and LucknowFOSS activity. Club-by-club recruitment and weekly activity remain unverified.

## 13. Location and travel — Official/map-derived
The campus coordinates are approximately 26.7983768, 81.0240627. Railway, airport, hospital and city-centre distances in `location.csv` are map-derived estimates requiring route verification.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — Anecdotal
The strongest repeated public theme is the coding/placement environment. The clearest risk theme is accommodation shortage and uncertainty. Independent evidence on mess, teaching, grading, senior-junior culture and complaint handling remains too limited for strong generalisation.

## 15. Parent Mode summary
The institute has a well-sourced identity, computing-focused programme set, competitive historical cutoffs and strong institution-published placement outcomes. Confirm the final cost, hostel arrangement, scholarship cycle and placement denominator before committing.

## 16. Student Mode summary
The institute appears attractive for coding, AI/software projects and computing branches. Carefully compare CS, IT, CSAI and CSB curricula, and verify hostel/commute reality and current club activity.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis
- **Hostel capacity and commute risk** — Official committee minutes document severe shortage and use of external accommodation; 2026 allotment is not confirmed.
- **Fee currency risk** — The detailed schedule visible through the current profile is older and subject to revision.
- **Placement denominator risk** — Brochure percentages are strong but do not publish the number of eligible or registered B.Tech students.
- **Infrastructure maturity risk** — The campus is modern but official records show continuing hostel construction and transport procurement.
- **Qualitative-evidence risk** — Recent independent student-experience evidence is limited and partly applicant-reported.
- **Branch-name interpretation** — All four programmes are computing-oriented but curricula and recruiter eligibility may differ; do not treat CS, IT, CSAI and CSB as interchangeable.
