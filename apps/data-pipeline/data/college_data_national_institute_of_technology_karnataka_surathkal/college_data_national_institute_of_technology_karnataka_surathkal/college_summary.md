# National Institute of Technology Karnataka, Surathkal — AdmitWise AI Research Summary

**Target admission year:** 2026  
**Research status:** Ready for human review  
**Completion:** 88%

## 1. Verified college overview — official verified

National Institute of Technology Karnataka, Surathkal, commonly called **NITK Surathkal**, is an autonomous Institute of National Importance established in 1960, formerly Karnataka Regional Engineering College. Its JoSAA institute code is **211**. The main campus is at Srinivasnagar, Surathkal, Mangaluru. [SRC001, SRC007]

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — official verified

NIRF Engineering ranks are 17 in 2025, 17 in 2024 and 12 in 2023. The 2025 NIRF submission reports no valid NAAC accreditation. Seven B.Tech. programmes are stated as NBA-accredited through 30 June 2028; MME and Mining require renewal verification after their listed June 2025 end date. [SRC008-SRC011, SRC015]

## 3. Admission routes — official verified

Indian B.Tech. admission is through JEE Main and JoSAA, with CSAB Special rounds for vacancies. The 2026 seat matrix uses Karnataka and Other Than Karnataka quotas. DASA/CIWG routes are separately documented. The current official profile states that branch change is not allowed. [SRC001]

## 4. Branches and intake — official verified

The 2026 matrix contains **11 B.Tech. programmes**, **956 regular seats** and **4 female supernumerary seats**. The quota/category split is retained in `college_profile.json`. [SRC001]

## 5. Historical cutoffs — official verified

`cutoffs.csv` contains **4,437 rows**: 4,230 JoSAA rows for 2023-2025 and 207 CSAB Special 2025 rows. Four JoSAA combinations with both ranks blank were excluded. Historical cutoffs are not labelled official 2026 cutoffs. [SRC002-SRC006]

## 6. Fees and total cost — official verified + calculated

The current 2026-27 B.Tech. tuition is ₹62,500 per semester. The calculated standard four-year hosteller gross schedule is **₹9,49,370**, comprising institutional charges plus Hostel Trust fees and mess advances. This is not an official published total and includes refundable/advance components. Full-remission and two-thirds-remission scenarios are separately calculated. [SRC001, SRC013, SRC014]

## 7. Scholarships — official verified / institution-published

Current strong evidence covers tuition remission, institute merit and merit-cum-means schemes. Donor/alumni and NSP schemes are retained as potentially applicable and require current deadline and benefit verification. No scholarship is represented as guaranteed. [SRC001, SRC016, SRC022]

## 8. Placement statistics — official verified / institution-published

NIRF records cover 2021-22 through 2023-24. The annual report gives a 2024-25 dated table with 755 eligible B.Tech. students and 581 placed, plus 418 internship slots. Its broad narrative claims and the JoSAA profile use different scope/date definitions; these are preserved rather than merged. [SRC011, SRC012, SRC018]

## 9. Recruiters — institution-published

Thirty companies explicitly named in the 2024-25 annual report are retained as confirmed for that year. Roles, branch eligibility, selection counts and packages remain null unless officially stated. [SRC012]

## 10. Company-to-skill guidance — general guidance

Ten role-category roadmaps are included. They are general career guidance, not verified NITK recruiter requirements.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — institution-published

The annual report states 13 boys' hostels with capacity 4,436 and six girls' hostels with capacity 1,335, while the JoSAA profile states 12 and five. The newer annual-report count is retained with a conflict flag. Medical, library, laboratory, sports, banking and campus-service information is captured without quality ratings. [SRC001, SRC012]

## 12. Clubs and student activities — institution-published

Twenty clubs/teams with dated 2024-25 activity evidence are listed, including Kannada Vedike, Music Club, Artists' Forum, BAJA NITK Racing, TEDxNITK, SPIC MACAY, Rotaract, Engineer and Incident. [SRC012, SRC019]

## 13. Location and travel — official verified

The campus is about 3 km from Surathkal Railway Station, 20 km from Mangalore International Airport and 22 km from Mangalore city/bus stand according to the official profile. Unverified coordinates and travel times remain null. [SRC001]

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — qualitative only

Nine public sources were retained. The strongest repeated positives concern campus life, clubs, beach access and peer environment. Hostel evidence is conflicting: older accounts are broadly positive about social life, while a recent incident-specific complaint alleges maintenance and water problems. No numeric culture rating was generated. [STU001-STU009]

## 15. Parent Mode summary

NITK has authoritative 2026 admission and fee data, strong historical placement evidence and significant tuition-remission routes. Parents should manually confirm current hostel allocation, scholarship deadlines, programme-level NBA renewal and final 2025-26 placement outcomes.

## 16. Student Mode summary

NITK is best suited to students valuing a residential coastal campus, active clubs, strong peer networks and broad recruiter exposure. Branch choice matters because the official profile states that branch change is not allowed, and software-role eligibility may differ by employer.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

- Historical 2023-25 cutoffs are not guaranteed 2026 cutoffs.
- Branch change is not permitted under the current official profile.
- Placement percentages differ by cohort, date and inclusion of higher studies.
- MME and Mining NBA renewal was not verified after June 2025.
- Hostel count and recent maintenance experience need current verification.
- Scholarship availability and deadlines can change each cycle.
