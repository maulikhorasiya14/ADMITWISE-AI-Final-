## 1. Verified college overview — Official verified

SRM Institute of Science and Technology is a private deemed-to-be university. The main engineering campus is at SRM Nagar, Kattankulathur/Potheri, Chengalpattu District, Tamil Nadu. The current official engineering admissions page lists **44 B.Tech programmes/specialisations and 5,760 seats** for Kattankulathur.

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — Official verified

- **NAAC:** A++, fourth cycle, valid 16 June 2024 to 15 June 2031.
- **NIRF Engineering:** rank 14, score 65.83 in 2025; rank 13, score 65.41 in 2024; rank 28, score 58.56 in 2023.
- **NIRF ID:** IR-E-U-0473.
- **NBA:** Current validity was verified only for selected base programmes. Specialisations do not inherit the base programme’s accreditation without an explicit programme code/letter.
- **AICTE:** The current approval letter and institute ID were not directly verified.

## 3. Admission routes — Official verified

The principal Indian route is **SRMJEEE 2026**, followed by online counselling and campus/programme selection. The official application page requires at least **60% aggregate in Physics, Mathematics and one eligible optional subject** and publishes three exam phases. The application fee is ₹1,400 per phase.

The counselling manual lists a one-time non-refundable registration fee of ₹10,000 and a ₹100,000 counselling payment adjusted against tuition. It must not be added twice.

Separate international/NRI/PIO/OCI and lateral-entry routes exist. No home-state quota, other-state quota, management quota, female supernumerary allocation or JEE Advanced route was inferred.

## 4. Branches and intake — Institution-published

All 44 current Kattankulathur programme rows are in `branches.csv`. The largest published intakes include:

- CSE: 1,140
- ECE: 480
- CSE AI & ML: 420
- Biotechnology: 300
- CSE Cyber Security: 300
- EEE and Mechanical: 240 each

The total 5,760 differs from NIRF’s 5,730 approved intake for 2024–25. This may reflect a later programme change and must be checked against the final 2026–27 AICTE approval.

## 5. Historical cutoffs — Unavailable

`cutoffs.csv` is intentionally header-only.

No public official branch-wise SRMJEEE closing-rank table meeting the required year, round, branch, category and quota schema was located for 2023, 2024 or 2025. The 2026 counselling process was not complete at the research date.

**Scholarship rank ranges are not admission cutoffs and were not inserted into `cutoffs.csv`.**

## 6. Fees and total cost — Official verified + calculated

Published annual tuition ranges from **₹2.75 lakh to ₹4.75 lakh**, depending on programme. The calculated no-escalation four-year tuition plus registration range is **₹11.10 lakh to ₹19.10 lakh**.

Published first-year hostel and mess totals among the reviewed options range from **₹1.10 lakh to ₹2.73 lakh**. Some boys’ options are explicitly off-campus.

A calculated first-year institutional range is approximately **₹3.95 lakh to ₹7.58 lakh**. This is not an official total and excludes personal expenses, future fee changes and optional laundry.

- Potheri suburban railway station is opposite/adjacent to the campus area.
- Chennai International Airport is approximately 24 km away; typical road travel can be around an hour and varies with traffic.

- SRM Medical College Hospital is on or next to the campus.
- Mahindra World City is comparatively nearby; OMR/Siruseri and central-city ecosystems require travel.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — Student-reported

### Strong repeated signal
Hostel experience varies substantially by block, price and room type. Popular rooms can fill quickly, and mess opinions conflict.

### Moderate repeated signals
- Broad campus life, clubs and events
- Strict attendance expectations
- Large cohorts and competitive access to opportunities
- Helpful seniors through clubs and peer networks
- Good rail connectivity but distance from central Chennai

### Conflicting signal
Aggregate official placements are strong, while students report substantial competition and limited branch/KTR-level transparency.

No numeric campus-culture score was generated.

## 15. Parent Mode summary — Perspective summary

The main strengths are scale, infrastructure, medical access, programme choice and aggregate placement reach. The main parent-facing risks are high total cost, scholarship uncertainty, very large intake, unavailable branch closing ranks and placement data that are not conclusively Kattankulathur-only.

## 16. Student Mode summary — Perspective summary

SRM KTR appears best suited to a self-driven student who will actively use technical clubs, projects, hackathons and internships. Exact branch/specialisation choice matters, especially for placement eligibility. Hostel block and attendance preferences should be checked before commitment.
