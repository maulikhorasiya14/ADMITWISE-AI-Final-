## 1. Verified college overview — Official verified / institution-published

BITS Pilani Hyderabad Campus is a private constituent campus of Birla Institute of Technology & Science, Pilani, a deemed-to-be university. It began its academic journey on 29 August 2008. The current official campus overview describes a fully residential 200-acre campus at Jawahar Nagar in the Shameerpet area with more than 6,200 students. [S001, S002]

## 2. Accreditation — Official verified, university-wide

BITS Pilani holds NAAC **A++**, CGPA **3.68**, cycle 4, with stated validity from 29 November 2024 to 28 November 2029. Its university-wide NIRF Engineering ranks were **11** in 2025, **20** in 2024 and **25** in 2023. [S017–S020]

Current Hyderabad-campus AICTE institute details and programme-wise NBA validity were not verified and remain null.

## 3. Admission routes — Official verified

Domestic First Degree admissions use BITSAT 2026. The published eligibility is at least 75% aggregate in the prescribed PCM/PCB subjects and at least 60% in each relevant subject. JEE Main and JEE Advanced are not used for this route. Ordinary home-state, category and management-quota allotments are not published. [S008–S010]

Other verified pathways include direct admission for eligible board toppers and International Student Admission. [S015, S016]

## 4. Branches and intake — Official verified / unavailable

Nine Hyderabad-campus B.E. programmes were verified for 2026-27: Chemical, Civil, Computer Science, Electronics and Communication, Electrical and Electronics, Electronics and Instrumentation, Mathematics and Computing, Mechanical, and Environmental and Sustainability Engineering. [S008]

No official branch-wise sanctioned intake or seat matrix was found. Intake fields remain null.

## 5. Historical cutoffs — Official verified

The package contains **24 official final BITSAT score records** for 2023–2025. These are scores out of 390, not ranks. BITS does not publish JoSAA-style opening/closing ranks or category, quota and gender-pool tables for these records. [S011]

- **2025:** CSE 270; MnC 266; ECE 256; EEE 239; E&I 232; Mechanical 214; Chemical 205; Civil 203; ESE 181.
- **2024:** CSE 298; MnC 293; ECE 284; EEE 275; E&I 270; Mechanical 251; Chemical 238; Civil 235.
- **2023:** CSE 284; ECE 265; EEE 251; E&I 244; Mechanical 218; Chemical 207; Civil 204.

Final official 2026 cutoffs are not included because the 2026 admission process had not concluded.

## 6. Fees and total cost — Official verified / calculated

The 2026-27 semester tuition is INR 291,500 and annual tuition is INR 583,000. The admission fee is INR 64,100, refundable caution deposit INR 3,000, scheduled annual hostel fee INR 72,000, and annual mess/electricity advance INR 20,000. [S012]

The calculated first-year billed/advanced outflow is **INR 766,775**. Scheduled four-year tuition is **INR 2,513,000**. No fixed all-inclusive four-year total is asserted because adjustable advances and future hostel/mess revisions are unavailable.

## 7. Scholarships — Official verified / potentially applicable

The official scholarship structure includes 100% tuition for the top 2% and 50% for the next 1% under semester merit criteria. MCN slabs provide 100%, 50%, 25% and 10% tuition waivers for specified bands. Top-500 BITSAT support can cover tuition for the normal four-year duration subject to income, CGPA and other conditions. [S013, S014]

All benefits are competitive and conditional. Current income documentation and deadlines require verification.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — Institution-published / student-reported

Current official pages describe a fully residential campus with single and double non-AC rooms, internet, security, common rooms, messes, sports, gym, library, laboratories, shops and a 24-hour medical system. [S003, S004, S021]

An older official page states six boys' and two girls' hostels, but the current page does not repeat those counts. Student sources commonly value the self-contained campus but mention summer heat, variable mess experience and occasional administrative or maintenance friction. [SX01, SX03, SX07, SX08]

## 12. Clubs and student activities — Institution-published

Eighteen official or institution-associated activities were retained. Recent public evidence is strongest for BITScan, the Computer Science Association and Synapsis. Other officially listed cultural and technical groups have incomplete current activity evidence. ATMOS, Pearl, Arena and Ignite are retained as historical/possibly dormant rather than assumed active. [S004, S023–S029]

## 13. Location and travel — Official / map-derived

The campus is in Jawahar Nagar/Shameerpet. The current overview gives approximately 27 km to Secunderabad and 70 km to Hyderabad airport. Official route 212 connects the Secunderabad area to campus. [S002, S022]

Map-derived travel estimates are about one hour to Secunderabad and 90–120 minutes to the airport, traffic-dependent. There is no direct metro. Students report that shared rides are common because solo cabs can be expensive or reluctant. [S031, SX04, SX05]

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — Student-reported

Repeated positives include modern infrastructure, residential peer life, technical associations, campus facilities and Practice School. Repeated concerns include high fees, remoteness, non-AC hostel heat, inconsistent mess experience, administrative friction and uncertainty about grading and recent placements. [SX01–SX13]

These are qualitative signals, not official facts or numeric ratings.

## 15. Parent Mode summary

Strong verified positives include official admission transparency, completed historical cutoffs, a campus-specific three-year placement table, established residential infrastructure and 24-hour medical support. Major risks are high cost, scholarship uncertainty, the recent placement-rate decline, missing branch-wise intake/outcomes and long city/airport travel.

## 16. Student Mode summary

The campus appears suited to students who value a large residential setting, clubs, academic autonomy, Practice School and access to Hyderabad's broader ecosystem. Students targeting a specific software role should verify branch eligibility rather than assume all recruiters accept all programmes.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

Key risks are high fees, uncertain scholarship realization, recent placement-rate decline, broad marketing language that differs from detailed results, missing branch-level placement data, software ambitions from a core branch, remote location, non-AC hostel heat and conflicting grading reports.

## 18. Data gaps
