# Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar — AdmitWise AI Research Summary

**Target admission year:** 2026  
**Research date:** 2026-06-26  
**Completion:** 93%  
**Status:** Ready for human review

## 1. Verified college overview — official verified
IIT Gandhinagar is a central-government statutory IIT and Institute of National Importance established in 2008. JoSAA institute code: **111**. The permanent residential campus is at Palaj, Gandhinagar, Gujarat.

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — official verified / unavailable
NIRF Engineering: **18 (2023, score 61.66), 18 (2024, score 63.42), 25 (2025, score 62.31)**. NIRF ID: **IR-E-U-0139**. Publicly verifiable AICTE, NAAC and programme-level NBA records were not established and remain null.

## 3. Admission routes — official verified
BTech admission is through JEE Advanced and JoSAA. IITs do not participate in CSAB Special rounds. The 2026 JoSAA profile publishes category reservation, female-supernumerary seats and branch-change conditions. Historical cutoffs are not 2026 guarantees.

## 4. Branches and intake — official verified
The 2026 matrix contains **8 BTech programmes, 288 regular seats and 72 female-supernumerary seats, total 360**. See `branches.csv` and `college_profile.json` for programme-level records.

## 5. Historical cutoffs — official verified dataset
The original verified cutoff dataset yielded **1,593 valid JoSAA rows**: 588 for 2023, 531 for 2024 and 474 for 2025. **4 blank/no-allotment placeholders** were excluded. Historical dual-degree programmes are retained only as historical records.

## 6. Fees and total cost — official verified plus calculated
The published first-semester full-fee total is **INR 176,400**. At unchanged current rates, the calculated four-year total is approximately **INR 1,308,650** for full tuition, **INR 726,516.67** for the one-third-tuition income band and **INR 435,450** for tuition-exempt categories. These are calculations, not fixed official totals.

## 7. Scholarships — official verified / institution-published
The package includes statutory tuition waivers, income-based remission, free basic messing for eligible SC/ST students, donor and excellence scholarships, Lakshmi Vadali, Chandrakant & Patricia Desai, and Farmson-IITGN support. Every award requires current official approval.

## 8. Placement statistics — official verified and institution-published
NIRF four-year UG outcomes: 140/188 placed with 16 LPA median (2022-23), 160/214 with 14 LPA median (2023-24), and 166/213 with 14 LPA median (2024-25). Annual reports separately state over 90%, 81% and 82.39% placement of registered UG students in their respective cycles; these scopes are not merged.

## 9. Recruiters — institution-published
Thirty organisations confirmed in the official 2024 annual placement list are recorded. Role, branch eligibility, package and selection counts remain null unless explicitly published.

## 10. Company-to-skill guidance — general career guidance
Roadmaps for software, analytics, electronics/VLSI and core engineering are included in `college_profile.json`. They are not company-specific verified requirements.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — institution-published
The current Student Affairs page lists **12 centrally air-conditioned hostels with capacity 2600+**, single/double rooms, Wi-Fi/LAN, power, security, laundry, pantries and two messes. Exact 2026 first-year occupancy requires confirmation. Medical centre and ambulance support are officially listed.

## 12. Clubs and student activities — institution-associated
Seventeen current cultural and technical clubs are structured in `clubs.csv`. Official governance shows active cultural, technical and sports councils; club-specific latest event dates are not uniformly available.

## 13. Location and travel — official and map-derived
Palaj campus is about 26 km from Ahmedabad airport. Gandhinagar Capital station is approximately 11.9 km away; Ahmedabad Junction is the major long-distance rail station. Dholakuva Circle is the nearest practical metro connection, requiring a final auto/cab leg.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — conflicting/limited
Common positives include infrastructure, faculty accessibility, research opportunities, academic flexibility and sports. Reported concerns include room-space changes, maintenance response, placement scale, campus isolation and uneven perceptions of club/coding intensity. No numeric campus-life rating is justified.

## 15. Parent Mode summary
Verified institutional standing and extensive aid are strengths. Confirm final fees, hostel allocation, scholarship award and placement scope before deciding.

## 16. Student Mode summary
Best suited to students valuing research, flexibility, modern residential infrastructure and leadership opportunities. Students focused narrowly on software outcomes should inspect branch fit and current recruiter eligibility.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis
Key risks are cutoff overinterpretation, denominator mismatch in placements, assumed scholarships, changing hostel occupancy and branch/software mismatch.
