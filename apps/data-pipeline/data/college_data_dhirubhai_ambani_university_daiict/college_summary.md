## 1. Verified college overview — Official verified

The current legal name is **Dhirubhai Ambani University (DAU)**. The previous and still widely used name is **Dhirubhai Ambani Institute of Information and Communication Technology (DA-IICT)**. The institution began academic operations in 2001 and is a private university established under Gujarat legislation. It is not a deemed university or an Institute of National Importance. The School of Technology operates from a 50-acre Gandhinagar campus. [S001–S003, S012]

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — Official verified

- UGC: private university; Section 2(f) recognition is institution-published. [S001, S003]
- NAAC: **A+**, CGPA **3.32/4**, cycle 2; stated validity 7 September 2023 to 6 September 2028. [S004]
- Government of Gujarat: Centre of Excellence. [S001]
- NIRF Engineering: rank band **201–300** in 2025 and 2024. DA-IICT participated in 2023 but was not found in the published ranked/rank-band list used for this package. [S005–S007]
- Current AICTE institute ID/applicability and programme-wise NBA validity: **unavailable**.

## 3. Admission routes — Official verified

### All India Category
Admissions use **JEE Main 2026 AIR** through DAU’s own online counselling. Candidates need Mathematics, Physics and one prescribed optional subject in Class XII, with the minimum percentage specified by AICTE. The application fee is INR 1,770 including GST. [S009]

### Gujarat Category
Seats are filled through **ACPC Gujarat** under current state rules. Candidates must complete the separate ACPC process. [S010]

### NRI and Foreign National / DAFS
A separate official route exists for eligible NRI, OCI, PIO and foreign-national applicants. [S011]

JEE Advanced is not required. No management quota or lateral-entry route was verified. B.Tech residence is officially compulsory. [S009, S012, S026]

## 5. Historical cutoffs — Official verified

The dataset contains **73 official ACPC final-closing records**:

Records cover OPEN, SC, ST, SEBC, EWS, ESM where allotted, and TFWS for ICT, ICT Honours, MnC and EVD. The official tables publish closing merit rank and merit marks/percentile; opening ranks are therefore null. [S013–S015]

No stable official archive of All India JEE Main closing ranks was found. No historical or predicted value is labelled an official 2026 cutoff.

## 6. Fees and total cost — Official verified / calculated

Published 2026 fee components for the domestic B.Tech route include:

- Tuition: **INR 192,500 per semester**
- Registration: **INR 2,500 per semester**
- Refundable caution deposit: **INR 25,000**
- Hostel: **INR 37,800 per semester**
- Food: actuals, approximately **INR 5,500 per month**

At the current rate, annual tuition is calculated as INR 385,000 and four-year tuition as INR 1,540,000. The current-rate first-year institutional outflow excluding food is calculated as INR 490,600. Fees are stated to be subject to an annual revision of 8–10%, so no fixed official four-year total is asserted. [S009]

EVD has additional published course charges of INR 18,000 after semester 2 and approximately INR 25,000 in semester 4. [S009]

## 7. Scholarships — Official verified / potentially applicable

The current institute scheme includes:

- Five full-semester Merit Scholarships for top students with SPI 8.5+
- Eight 70% Merit-cum-Means awards for SPI 6.5+ and family income below INR 6 lakh
- Twenty 50% Merit-cum-Means awards for SPI 6.5+ and family income below INR 8 lakh
- Fifteen full-tuition Institute Fellowships divided among top JEE/ACPC ranks, other-state board toppers and eligible girl students
- Cybage Khushboo assistance: 80% tuition or INR 150,000 per year, whichever is lower, subject to detailed eligibility
- Potential government schemes through MYSY, Digital Gujarat and the National Scholarship Portal

Awards are competitive, conditional and not guaranteed. Jai Jhulelal and Satnaam WaheGuruji schemes are retained as historical cohort-specific records requiring 2026 confirmation. [S016–S022, S040–S043]

## 13. Location and travel — Official / map-derived

The campus is on DA-IICT Road in Gandhinagar, near Infocity. Gandhinagar Capital Railway Station is approximately 9 km away; Ahmedabad airport is approximately 18 km away. Infocity is nearby and GIFT City is reachable by road. These route figures are map-derived and traffic-dependent. [S001, S037]

Aashka Multispeciality Hospital is officially described as adjacent/reachable within ten minutes, with a separate Apollo Gandhinagar arrangement. [S029]

## 15. Parent Mode summary

The institution has strong official evidence for admissions, ACPC cutoffs, residential infrastructure, scholarship structures and audited placement reporting. Main risks are annual fee escalation, placement-scope confusion, lack of official All India historical cutoffs, non-AC compulsory hostels and missing branch-wise placement outcomes.

## 16. Student Mode summary

DAU appears best suited to students seeking rigorous ICT/computing/electronics education, a strong coding peer group, a residential campus and student-run activities. Students considering CS-AI or ECE-AI should recognise that these new 2026 programmes have no graduate placement history.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

Key decision risks are:

1. High fees with 8–10% published annual revision
2. Confusing NIRF whole-cohort and audited eligible-pool placement rates
3. No official historical All India JEE closing-rank archive
4. No placement history for the two new programmes
5. Scholarship dependence without guaranteed renewal
6. Demanding academic workload
7. Non-AC compulsory residence
8. Potential mismatch between branch and target role
9. Conflicting language-diversity experiences
