## 1. Verified college overview — Official verified

National Institute of Technology, Warangal (NITW), formerly Regional Engineering College Warangal, was established in 1959. It is a centrally funded Institute of National Importance. The JoSAA institute code is **228**. The main campus is in Hanumakonda District, Telangana, PIN 506004.

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — Official verified / unavailable

- NIRF Engineering 2025: rank **28**, score **61.05**.
- NIRF Engineering 2024: rank **21**, score **61.72**.
- NIRF Engineering 2023: rank **21**, score **61.13**.
- The JoSAA profile says all B.Tech programmes are NBA accredited, but programme-wise validity dates were not independently verified.
- Current NAAC status and an AICTE institute ID were not verified and remain null.

## 3. Admission routes — Official verified

Regular undergraduate admission is through JEE Main and JoSAA. Home State and Other State quotas, category reservations and PwD pools are represented in the official matrix. CSAB Special rounds fill eligible vacancies. DASA-CIWG and DASA-Non CIWG combinations appear in the 2025 CSAB documents. The JoSAA profile says branch change after first year is not permitted.

## 4. Branches and intake — Official verified

The 2026 matrix contains 12 B.Tech programmes, two five-year dual-degree programmes and three five-year integrated M.Sc. programmes. The matrix totals **1,049 seats** across all listed programmes; B.Tech programmes account for **968 seats**. The structured branch table contains 14 engineering branch/dual-degree records.

## 6. Fees and total cost — Official verified / calculated

The JoSAA 2026 profile displays standard B.Tech semester tuition of INR 62,500. It shows a current day-scholar total of INR 105,000 and hosteller total of INR 148,000, with a hostel component of INR 43,000 and other fee of INR 42,500. The displayed hostel component's period requires confirmation before annualizing. The INR 500,000 four-year tuition value is a calculated eight-semester estimate, not an official all-inclusive total.

SC/ST/PwD students are shown with zero tuition, while other and hostel charges remain payable. Final 2026-27 fee and refund rules must be checked in the institute's final circular.

## 7. Scholarships — Official verified / potentially applicable

The package records institute merit, merit-cum-means, donor/alumni assistance, the Endowment Fund, IMPACT99 and National Scholarship Portal possibilities. The 2025 donor-support call mentions assistance up to INR 50,000 per year. Historical or call-based schemes are not guaranteed for 2026; current deadlines and selection criteria require verification.

## 8. Placement statistics — Official verified / institution-published

The structured placement file contains **21** records. The 2024-25 placement-cell table reports 697 placements among 806 eligible B.Tech participants. Branch results differ substantially: CSE, ECE and EEE should not be used as proxies for all programmes. NIRF cohort figures and placement-cell eligibility figures are preserved separately because they use different scopes.

Thirty organisations from the official 2024-25 placement brochure are included. A brochure listing does not prove that a company currently visits, nor does it establish a role, branch eligibility or package; those fields remain null unless officially published.

## 10. Company-to-skill guidance — General career guidance

The profile includes role-category roadmaps for software, data, VLSI, embedded, electrical, mechanical, civil, chemical, materials and consulting/operations careers. These are general preparation suggestions, not verified NITW company requirements.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — Institution-published

Official material describes approximately 248 acres, 16 residential blocks, three girls' hostels, one foreign-student hostel and capacity around 5,500. It also lists multiple messes, a central library, sports facilities, medical/ambulance support and grievance mechanisms. Hostel entitlement is an open conflict: the hostel page suggests accommodation for admitted students, while hostel rules say it cannot be claimed as a right.

## 12. Clubs and student activities — Institution-associated

Twenty officially listed technical, cultural, service and wellness groups are included. Because most listings are static and no recent activity was independently confirmed for every club, activity status is marked **unable to verify** rather than automatically active.

## 13. Location and travel — Official verified / unavailable

Kazipet Junction is approximately 2 km from campus and Warangal railway station approximately 10 km. Hyderabad's international airport is approximately 175 km away. Coordinates, typical travel times, hospital distance and city-centre distance remain null until map verification.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — Student-reported / anecdotal

Public accounts indicate a strong residential peer environment and broad campus activity. Hostel maintenance, occupancy and hygiene appear to vary by block. Academic workload and exam cadence receive mixed reports. Evidence is self-selected and insufficient for numeric culture scores.

## 15. Parent Mode summary — Perspective summary

The institute offers strong official academic and placement evidence, especially in circuit branches, with useful rail access and a large residential campus. Parents should verify final fees, hostel entitlement, programme accreditation and scholarship continuation before committing.

## 16. Student Mode summary — Perspective summary

Branch fit is critical. Students targeting software roles from core branches should verify company eligibility and build DSA/development skills early. The campus has many official clubs and facilities, but hostel experience varies and current club recruitment should be checked directly.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis

- Branch-level placement differences can be large.
- Hostel allotment and block quality require verification.
- Fee periodicity prevents a reliable four-year all-inclusive calculation.
- Newer programmes have less historical placement/accreditation evidence.
- Scholarship continuation and awards are uncertain.
- Hyderabad technology exposure requires substantial travel.
