# ABV-IIITM Gwalior — AdmitWise AI College Intelligence Package

**Research date:** 2026-06-26  
**Target admission year:** 2026  
**Status:** Ready for human review

## 1. Verified college overview — Official verified
Atal Bihari Vajpayee Indian Institute of Information Technology and Management, Gwalior is a centrally funded autonomous Institute of National Importance established in 1997. The JoSAA institute code is **301** and the main campus is on Morena Link Road, Gwalior.

## 2. Accreditation and ranking — Official verified / unavailable
NIRF Engineering: **2025 rank 96 (score 46.25)**; **2024 rank band 101–150**; **2023 rank 88 (score 43.41)**. Current NAAC, AICTE applicability and programme-wise NBA validity remain unverified and are left null.

## 3. Admission routes — Official verified
UG/integrated admission is through JEE Main and JoSAA, followed by CSAB Special for vacancies. DASA/CIWG applies under current rules. The 2026 matrix uses All India seats and has no home-state quota.

## 4. Branches and intake — Official verified
The 2026 JoSAA matrix contains five programmes and **360 seats**: CSE 90, MSC 60, EEE 60, IMT 90 and IMG 60. Female-only pools are regular seat pools; JoSAA reports **zero female-supernumerary seats**.

## 5. Historical cutoffs — Official verified
The package contains **1099** validated cutoff rows: **1026 JoSAA** and **73 CSAB Special**. CSAB counts are Round 1 = 44, Round 2 = 16, Round 3 = 13. JoSAA category ranks and CSAB All India CRL are kept separate. No official 2026 cutoff has been created.

## 6. Fees and total cost — Institution-published / calculated
The official 2026 circular charges regular institute fees of ₹105,000 and hostel/mess fees of ₹35,000 per semester, plus one-time/security payments. Calculated published-cost totals are ₹11,48,750 for a regular four-year B.Tech. and ₹3,88,750 for a tuition-waiver B.Tech. category. Five-year and DASA calculations are separately labelled; personal expenses are excluded.

## 7. Scholarships — Official/institution-published
The January 2026 MCM policy provides 10 Type-I awards (₹1,000/month plus tuition exemption) and 41 Type-II awards (₹36,000/year), subject to merit, income up to ₹8 lakh, CGPA and other conditions. Government and external schemes are listed only as potentially applicable and are not guaranteed.

## 8. Placement statistics — Official verified and institution-published
NIRF records separate four-year UG and five-year integrated cohorts for 2022-23 to 2024-25. For 2024-25 it reports 73/73 UG students placed at a ₹24.70 LPA median and 89/104 integrated students placed at a ₹19.63 LPA median. The institute brochure separately reports programme averages and a 90% eligibility-based placement claim; these scopes are not merged.

## 9. Recruiters — Institution-published
Thirty 2024-25 brochure-listed recruiters are included. Roles, selection counts, branch eligibility, CGPA requirements and packages are null unless officially stated.

## 10. Company-to-skill guidance — General career guidance
General roadmaps are provided for software, data, electronics and consulting/product roles. They are not college-specific company requirements.

## 11. Hostels and facilities — Institution-published
The 2025-26 brochure reports four boys hostels, one girls hostel and 1,700+ occupancy. Hostel and mess are described as compulsory; 10 PM attendance and late-entry recording are documented. Sports infrastructure, library, health centre and a 24x7 ambulance are reported.

## 12. Clubs and student activities — Institution-associated
Ten councils, forums, publications and active event teams are recorded, including SAC, AASF, IEEE Student Branch, Abhishar, INFOTSAV, Aurora, URJA and the Inter-IIIT organising team.

## 13. Location and travel — Official / map-derived
JoSAA reports the campus at approximately 7 km from Gwalior Junction and 10 km from the airport, with road, cab and auto access. Coordinates, travel times and hospital distance are map-derived estimates requiring manual verification.

## 14. Student-reported campus reality — Anecdotal / conflicting
Public accounts commonly praise computing peers and fest life, but placement scope, EEE outcomes, integrated-programme policies, hostel quality and branch workload receive mixed reports. No anonymous accusation is treated as fact.

## 15. Parent Mode summary
Identity, seats, fees and NIRF cohorts are strongly sourced. Confirm scholarship awards, current branch-specific placements, hostel rules and final five-year cost before admission decisions.

## 16. Student Mode summary
Strongest fit appears to be for students prioritising computing, mathematics, coding and a residential campus. EEE and IMG/IMT applicants should verify curriculum, exit policy and software-company eligibility directly.

## 17. Decision Risk Analysis
- **Branch-outcome mismatch (high for EEE-focused decisions):** Official placement history mainly covers mature computing and integrated cohorts; EEE is newer and branch-specific outcomes are not yet available.
- **Placement-scope confusion (medium):** NIRF cohort medians, brochure averages and a 90% eligibility-based claim measure different populations and metrics.
- **Residential-rule mismatch (medium for students wanting flexible off-campus life):** Hostel and mess are described as compulsory, with 10 PM attendance and late-entry recording.
- **Fee revision and scholarship uncertainty (medium):** The 2026 circular is subject to revision; MCM and government schemes are competitive and not guaranteed.
- **Integrated-degree fit (medium):** Five-year IMT/IMG programmes have different curricula and duration; exit options and current policy require direct confirmation.
- **Local ecosystem scale (low-to-medium depending on preference):** Gwalior provides a lower-cost residential environment but less local industry density than major technology hubs.
